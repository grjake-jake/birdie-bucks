❓ How To Play
Birdie Bucks
💰 Buck Skins
Each hole is worth a set dollar amount. The player with the lowest score on a hole wins that amount from every other player. If two or more players tie for the lowest score, nobody wins — the hole is a tie.
Carry-Overs (optional): When a hole is tied, the dollar value carries over and adds to the next hole. Ties keep stacking until someone wins a hole outright.
Birdie Doubles (optional): If a player wins a hole with a birdie or better, the pot doubles. A $3 pot becomes $6 from every other player.
Honors (optional): The player with the lowest score on a hole collects an additional set amount from every other player, separate from the skins pot.
2️⃣ Back 9 Doubles
Plays exactly like Buck Skins but the stakes automatically double on the back 9. If the front 9 bet is $1 per hole, every hole on the back 9 is worth $2. Carry-overs and birdie doubles still apply on top of the doubled value.
Great for keeping the back 9 exciting when the front 9 results are already decided.
📈 Escalating Stakes
The bet amount increases every 6 holes. You set three dollar amounts before the round — one for holes 1-6, one for holes 7-12, and one for holes 13-18. Each tier can be a different amount.
Example: holes 1-6 at $0.25, holes 7-12 at $0.50, holes 13-18 at $1.00.
Carry-overs and birdie doubles still apply. A carry-over crossing into a new tier uses the new tier dollar amount.
📊 Stable Stakes
A points-based game using the Stableford scoring system. Players earn points based on their score relative to par on each hole. The player with the most points at the end wins.
Eagle or better: 4 points
Birdie: 3 points
Par: 2 points
Bogey: 1 point
Double bogey or worse: 0 points
Playing Together
One player starts the game and gets a room code. Share that code with your playing partners. Each player enters the code to join and can enter their own scores on their own phone.
Players can join mid-round. The host can also add players and adjust game settings during the round.
